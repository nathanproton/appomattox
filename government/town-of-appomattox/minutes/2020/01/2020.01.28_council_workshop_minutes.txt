1  Appomattox Town Council

Workshop Meeting
January 28, 2020

The Appomattox Town Council held a Workshop meeting on Tuesday, January 28, 2020 at 6:00
p.m. at the Appomattox Municipal Building, 210 Linden Street, Appomattox, Virginia with
Mayor Harvey, presiding.

Town Council members present were Jonathan D. Garrett, James Boyce, Sr., Mary Lou Spiggle,
Timothy W. Garrett, Steven T. Conner and Claudia G. Puckette.

Staff present were Town Manager, Gary Shanaberger; Town Treasurer, Kim Ray; and Town
Clerk, Roxanne Casto.

Mayor Harvey called the meeting to order.

On a motion by Mrs. Puckette, seconded by Mr. Conner, Council voted to write off accounts;
Personal Property taxes - $6,895.04; Real Estate taxes $143.85; Utility invoices - $1,800.83;
Miscellaneous Accounts Receivable (bankruptcy) - $13,895.86 for a total of $22,735.58 that
have reached the statute of limitations for collection.  All members present voting aye.  Motion
carried 6-0.

On a motion by Mr. Jonathan Garrett, seconded by Mr. Timothy Garrett, Council voted on the
following resolution:

1.  Consideration to adopt a resolution amending the FY 2020 Operating Budget for the General

Fund for fireworks

the budget from time to time

amend the FY 2020 Operating Budget for the General Fund.

Council Standing Committee Reports – None

Council Comments – None

Town Staff Reports:
Mrs. Ray presented the Treasurer’s Report for December 2019.

Mr. Elder – not present – none

Mrs. Casto – none

Mr. Shanaberger reported on the following items:

•  Staff continues to work on multiple projects.
•  The Inflow & Infiltration (I&I) project is expected to be placed out to bid

in the next two (2) months.

•  Design of the waterline replacement on Church Street is in progress.  The

Town is hopeful to have plans by June 1st.

•  The Downtown Revitalization project is on-going.
•  The Town is expecting Lee Grant Avenue drawings any day.
•  Staff has started the FY 2021 annual budget preparations.
•  The EDA and Mr. Shanaberger are planning to travel to Marion, Virginia
on Friday, March 27, 2020 to discuss their Main Street renovations and
pop up program.

•  The Council Retreat scheduled for Friday, February 7, 2020 may need to
be rescheduled due to a scheduling conflict.  More information will be
forthcoming.

Adjournment:
On a motion by Mr. Boyce, seconded by Mr. Timothy Garrett, Council voted to adjourn at 6:45
p.m. All members present voting aye. Motion carried 5-0.
