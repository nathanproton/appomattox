content: The regular meeting of the Appomattox Town Council was held on Monday, June 17, 1974 at 7:30 P.M. with Vice-Mayor Mrs. Earle Robinson presiding.

Members present were Martin Webb, Ivey Holland, Burke Guill, R. C. Hubbard, and T. E. Conner.

The meeting was opened with a prayer.

The minutes of the last meeting were read and approved.

The minutes of a Building and Property Committee meeting were read.

Mr. Slagle gave a brief summary of the agreement between the Town of Appomattox and the Arts and Crafts Coop for their use of the Depot.

Mr. Slagle explained that the Water Quality Management Plan will now have to be approved by the Towns also.

Mr. Hubbard reported the Junior Woman's Club would like to co-sponsor a "Historic Appomattox Railroad Festival" for the weekend of October 5th and 6th. On a motion by R. C. Hubbard, seconded by Martin Webb, Council voted to refer this request to the Building & Property Committee for their recommendation. Mr. Hubbard as Chairman of the Committee, set the tentative date for June 25, 1974 at 7:30 P.M.

5 Ayes 0 Nays

Mr. Conner asked what was being done on a complaint by a citizen of the Town about the sawmill on Lee-Grant Avenue. Mr. Slagle explained he was working with the citizen and the Sawmill operators to come to a "workable" arrangement.

Conner made a motion to place "Slow, Children at Play" signs in the necessary locations on Lee-Grant Avenue, seconded by Martin L. Webb.

Ayes 5 Nays 0

Mr. Hubbard made the motion, seconded by T. E. Conner to adopt the following resolution:
