1  Workshop Council Meeting
Tuesday, June 23, 2009

The Appomattox Town Council held a workshop council meeting on Tuesday, June 23, 2009 at
7:00 pm at the Appomattox Municipal Building, 210 Linden Street, Appomattox, Virginia with
Mayor Harvey presiding.

Members present:  Bryan J. Baine, N.H. “Jimmy” Mayberry, John T. “Plicky” Williams, Karl E.
Carter, Jennifer Jamerson-Scruggs.  Absent:  C. Lewis McDearmon, Jr.

Others:  Carlton Duck, Derek Norfield, Barbara Shelton, Mary Lou Spiggle, Nina Beasley, Chad
Millner, Bart S. Van Nieuwenhuise, Town Manager and Roxanne Paulette, Town Clerk.

Mayor Harvey called the meeting to order.

SECTION IV:
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Appomattox Town Council hereby establishes the
property tax rate at $.13 per $100.00 of assessed value for real estate, $.55 per $100.00 of
assessed value for personal property, $.55 per $100.00 of assessed value for machinery and tools,
and $.13 per $100.00 of assessed value for mobile homes; and,

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, in accordance with the requirements set forth in Section 58.1-
3524(C)(2) and Section 58.1-3912(E) of the Code of Virginia, as amended by Chapter 1 of the
Acts of Assembly (2004 Special Session 1) and as set forth in Item 503.E (Personal Property Tax
Relief Program) of Chapter 951 of the 2005 Acts of Assembly any qualifying vehicle sitused
within the Town commencing January 1, 2006, shall receive personal property tax relief in the
following manner:

•  Personal use vehicles valued at $1,000 or less will be eligible for 100% tax relief;
•  Personal use vehicles valued at $1,001 to $20,000 will be eligible for 60% tax relief;
•  Personal use vehicles valued at $20,001 or more shall only receive 60% tax relief on the

first $20,000 of value; and

•  All other vehicles which do not meet the definition of “qualifying” (business use

•

vehicles, farm use vehicles, motor homes, etc.) will not be eligible for any form of tax
relief under this program.
In accordance with Item 503.D.1., the entitlement to personal property tax relief for
qualifying vehicles for tax year 2005 and all prior tax years shall expire on September 1,
2006.  Supplemental assessments for tax years 2005 and prior that are made on or after
September 1, 2006 shall be deemed “non-qualifying” for the purposes of state tax relief
and the local share due from the taxpayer shall represent 100% of the tax assessable.

The discussion of the “draft” Lease Law will be referred to the Ordinance Committee for review.

The discussion of the “draft” Urban Forestry Ordinance will be advertised for a public hearing to
be held on July 13, 2009.

Standing Committee reports –
Finance Committee – none

Fire & Safety Committee – Met to discuss and review the content for the July 2009 Newsletter.

Ordinance Committee – none

Personnel – The committee met on June 22, 2009 and the Treasurer’s position will be filed July
1, 2009 pending the Background and Criminal checks.  The Secretary II position will be filed by
July 1, 2009 and the deadline for applications for the Utility II position is June 30, 2009.

Street & Property Committee – Mr. Baine reported that the committee has met and discussed the
Tree Board Ordinance, the Pavilion at Abbitt Park.  The committee will meet to discuss the
action plan of the Sidewalk Master Plan.

Utility Committee – none

Staff Reports –
Mr. Van Nieuwenhuise, Town Manager gave various department head reports.

Council Concerns –
Mr. Carter and Mr. Williams – Commended the Utility Department for repairing the water leak
on S. Church Street.  The blacktop is very smooth.

Mr. Baine – Is very pleased with the number of people coming forward to help with the 37th
Annual Railroad Festival.

On a motion by Councilmember Carter, seconded by Councilmember Jamerson-Scruggs,
Council voted to adjourn at 7:32 pm.  All members present voting aye.  Motion carried.
